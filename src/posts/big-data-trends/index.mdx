## Big data

term big data refers to data that is large enough that it becomes difficult or impossible to process data in traditional methods. Accessing and storing large
amounts of information has been around for long time. But these big voluminous datasets can be used to address business problems you wouldn’t have been able to
tackle before. Big data can be described by the following characteristics. It also known as 4 v’s of big data.

1. **Volume:** The big data itself is related to size which is enormous. Size of data plays a very crucial role in determining value out of data. That’s why volume is an important factor in dealing with big data solutions.
1. **Variety:** Data comes in all kinds of formats such as structured, numeric data, unstructured data, emails, photo, video, documents, transactions, IOT etc. Unstructured data requires additional preprocessing while utilizing the data.
1. **Velocity:** Velocity means the speed of generation of data. Normally, the highest velocity of data streams directly into memory versus being written to disk. There are lot of products that depends on real-time integrations which means it is depending on the velocity.
1. **Variability:** Another new term added in the big data world is variability. This refers to the inconsistency which can be shown by the data at different times, thus interrupting the process of being able to handle and manage data.

Recent technological breakthroughs have massively reduced cost of data storage, data add, and updates made it less expensive. To make statistically backed business
decision people need to use these data lakes. But finding the right analytics solution does not only depend on these data, but it also depends on businesses, executive
asking the right questions and implement in their business. It started at 2005, when people started realizing how much data a platform like Facebook can generate. Hadoop
and NoSQL started gaining popularity at that time. With the advent of IOT devices, more devices are connecting to internet which is gathering a massive amount of data to
analyze usage patterns and make critical business decisions.

## Big-Data use cases

-   **Product development:** Companies like Netflix, Amazon, Audible use big data to predict customer demand. They build predictive models for new product or before launching new features by classifying user behavior. Google AdWords is also using excessive amount of data from google search history and using these to integrate AdWords Campaign.
-   **Customer experience:** A clearer view of customer experience has been so important like now. Big data enables to gather data from social media, websites, call logs, public CCTV footages to improve interaction experience. Reducing customer churn by delivering personalized offers has been improved rapidly.
-   **Fraud and compliance:** By help of big data there has been massive improvement in the fraud detection system. Financial institutions use customer previous data to analyze their behavior which helps to identify potential fraudulent transactions.
-   **Machine learning:** Machine learning is a hot topic right now. Machine learning algorithms were developed long before we have been introduced to big data. Because of lack of power in our computers and most importantly lack of data was the reason machine learning field took so long to shine despite it started at the 90s.
-   **Operational efficiency:** Operational efficiency may not always make the new but it’s an area in which big data is having the most impact. It helps to improve decision making in the line with current market demand.

## Challenges

While big data holds lot of promises but it’s not without its challenges. Although new technologies have been constantly developed, organizations are finding it hard to keep pace with the growth of data. It’s not just about storing data; it should also be available to perform and analyze fast enough to meet the business needs. Data scientists spend 50% to 80% of their time in data cleaning and preparing for machine learning algorithm. Finally, big data technology is changing at a rapid pace. A few years ago, Apache Hadoop was the popular technology used to handle big data. Then Apache spark and Apache Cassandra was introduced to solve the problems in Hadoop, to facilitate the challenges businesses faced at that time. Keeping up with the big data technology is an ongoing challenge.

## How it works

Big data gives new insights that opens new opportunities and business models. But jumping a big data solution straight ahead will not be fruitful. It needs to consider how it flows among multitude of locations, sources, systems, and users. Five key aspects are very important before tacking a big data solution.

-   **Setting up a strategy:** At a higher level, big data strategy is a plan designed to help oversee and improve the way you acquire, store, manage and use data within and outside of your organization. This calls for treating big data like any other valuable business asset rather than just a by-product of applications.
-   **Understand the source of data:** Understanding the source of the data is very important when designing a big data solution. It can be integrated from streaming data, social media data, publicly available data, others.
-   **Access, manage and store:** Companies should ensure the methods of integrating data, ensure data quality and maintain data governance. There are less expensive cloud solutions like Hadoop, Apache Cassandra, Apache Spark.
-   **Analyze Data:** Big data analytics is how companies gain value and insights from data. Increasingly, big data feeds today’s advanced analytics such as AI.
-   **Make intelligent decisions:** Well managed and trusted data leads to valuable business decisions. The more businesses can get out of their data, the more chance of increasing the business growth.

## Amazon DynamoDB

Among lots of big data solutions, I am going to discuss about AWS DynamoDB. Amazon DynamoDB[4] is a fully managed NoSQL[5] database service that makes it simple and cost effective to store and retrieve any amount of data, serve any level of request traffic. It stores structured data in tables, indexed by primary key and allows low latency read and write access to items ranging from 1 byte to 400kb which supports three types of data types (number, string, binary). But with these building blocks you can create String, Number, Boolean, Binary, Null, String set, Number Set, Binary Set, List, Map etc. Like MongoDB[6], DynamoDB also doesn’t depend on fixed schema, each data can have different attributes. Primary key can be a single attribute hash key or a composite hash key.

### How DynamoDB Stores Data:

DynamoDB supports two data models allowing more flexibility in building the database. Firstly, it stores data as key-value pair hash table. Items in the table can be identified uniquely by their hash values which is used to do GET, SET, UPDATE, DELETE. DynamoDB has two types of attributes, one is primary key, and another is sort key. Primary key helps to identify the item and sort key helps to sort the query result. But hash table has a limitation, it can only retrieve one record at a time. To solve this problem, DynamoDB has a second structure named as wide column store, it defines as each row can have any number of columns. This can be used to reference and order by different primary keys and sort keys.

### Features

-   Scaling up and down a database is very easy in DynamoDB. Without manual scaling it can auto scale database based on requests without any server downtime.
-   DynamoDB provides on-demand backup capability. It allows you to create full backups of your tables for long-term retention and archival for regulatory compliance needs. You can create on-demand backups and enable point-in-time recovery for your Amazon DynamoDB tables.
-   Point in time recovery: These is a very cool feature. You can revert to any time if there’s a business need or mistaken entry in database. It can retain up-to 35 days of data recovery.
-   It helps remove the expired files automatically to reduce the storage space[7].
-   One of the main advantages of using DynamoDB is it can integrate to any AWS services easily.
-   AWS has a great free tier options for the developers which helps to test out the resources.
-   DynamoDB is both highly scalable and elastic. There’s no limit of how much data you can store in it, the service automatically allocates more storage as you write more data. You can manually adjust the scaling capacity based on need[12].

### Use cases:

-   Application with Large amount of dataset: As a company’s data grows, JOINs and advanced SQL queries can be slow down. With DynamoDB queries have predictable latency up to any size including over 100TBS[8].
-   Most popular use case of DynamoDB is using it with AWS Lambda. Both lambda and DynamoDB supports auto scaling which makes it easy to main and development faster because developers can spend more time on business logic rather than maintaining servers and data.
-   Datasets with known access patterns: DynamoDB’s simple key-value pair pair make it fast and reliable.
-   Examples: One of the reason people can hesitate to use DynamoDB is they might be uncertain about it fitting their project. Some of the popular apps using DynamoDB are Duolingo, Major League Baseball, Hess Corporation, GE Healthcare, Docomo[9]. Other companies like Snapchat[10], Dropbox[11] saved millions of dollars using DynamoDB to a certain extent.
